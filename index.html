Areas of Interest

Authentic Leadership

Purpose, heart, integrity, and relationships form the core of good leadership. I believe in empowering teams and building trust that our successes and good failures represent opportunities to learn as we solve wicked problems for our students, our communities, and ourselves. Putting in the hard work alongside colleagues and teams through boring, persistent efforts to ensure access, completion, and success is effective, meaningful, and a force-multiplier that yields enduring results while responsibly stewarding tax and tuition dollars.

Early Academic Momentum

Building on my doctoral research regarding part-time community college students, I am dedicated to identifying and removing curricular barriers that stall student progress in their first year. My interest involves partnering with faculty to implement effective supports and strategies to increase engagement in "gateway" courses like mathematics and English. This approach prioritizes academic rigor while utilizing data to identify specific interventions that improve retention and completion rates.

Program Alignment with Local Need

My approach to program development is grounded in rigorous market analysis to ensure academic offerings align with regional economic ecosystems and target occupations. Leveraging my background in statistical consulting and stakeholder engagement, I evaluate new and existing programs for their ability to deliver family-sustaining wages to support local communities. This ensures the college fulfills its mission by creating clear, high-value pathways from the classroom to the workforce while responsibly stewarding tax dollars.

Brief Bio

Dr. Matt Wiley is a tenured Associate Professor of Mathematics and an experienced higher education administrator who bridges the gap between classroom pedagogy and institutional strategy. Currently serving as the Director of Institutional Effectiveness and Learning Technologies at Victoria College, Matt serves as the principal architect for regional accreditation and strategic planning, directly supporting executive leadership in driving student success initiatives and distance education.

With a deep commitment to shared governance, Matt previously chaired the Curriculum and Instructional Council and served as a Faculty Senator, where he championed faculty development and academic quality. As a professor, he ranked in the 90th percentile for teaching effectiveness and was named Advisor of the Year, demonstrating his dedication to the student experience.

Matt brings a unique, data-informed perspective to academic leadership. He holds a Doctor of Education in Higher Education Administration and Leadership, a Master of Business Administration, a Master of Science in Pure Mathematics, and has presented alongside the Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board on financial forecasting and Texas House Bill 8 funding models. Outside the academy, he was the managing partner of a statistical consultancy and co-authored three books on the R programming language. Matt leverages technical expertise to demystify complex data for stakeholders, ensuring that curriculum development and program evaluation support Texan communities through both innovation and sustainability.
